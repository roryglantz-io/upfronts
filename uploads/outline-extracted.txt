Upfronts Newsletter Outline 
Market Overview — Spend projections, upfront vs. scatter, key players 
Streaming growth is actually faster than last year projected (+18.9% vs. +14%). Linear decline is slightly less severe than projected (-4.5% vs. -7%) but accelerating again in 2026.
CTV &amp; Streaming — Ad-supported growth, share of upfront spend
84% of U.S. households subscribed to at least one major streaming service by end of 2024
45% of ad-supported video viewing now takes place on CTV (A18-49)
In 2025, the average consumer uses 10.9 streaming services per month (paid + free) vs 8 in 2024.
44% of consumers subscribe to at least one bundle (e.g., Disney+/Hulu/Max)
Streaming's share of 18-49 video time: from just 2% in 2014 → 45% in 2024
Streaming TV ad sales: +18.9% in 2025, projected +16.4% in 2026
Streaming reached $12.9bn in ad sales in 2025 vs. Linear at $34.0bn — but the gap is closing fast
Netflix and Amazon now account for ~30% of streaming ad sales — and growing fast. Netflix doubled its upfront commitments for 2025-26 
Back in 2021, CTV was 1/5 of total upfront spending — now expected to be 1/2 of total upfront spend
Streaming AVOD tiers carry only 3–8 minutes of ads per hour — a major differentiator for viewer experience
Ad-Supported Growth 
Willingness to watch ads in exchange for lower costs increased to 62% in 2025 (from 58% in 2024) (Kantar)
Ad-supported streaming tiers have expanded significantly — the pricing gap between AVOD and SVOD continues to widen, pushing more viewers into ad-supported tiers
78% of Peacock viewers and 73% of Prime Video viewers are on ad-supported tiers
Linear TV &amp; Cable — Reach, viewership declines, cord-cutting trends
Magna Data 
Linear TV usage peaked at 31+ hours/week in 2008-2009 = now down to 6.5 hours/week in 2024 
Linear TV weekly reach fell from 93% in 2011 --&gt; 68% in 2024 
Cable network’s weekly reach dropped from 54% to 30% in the last five years 
Median age of broadcast prime-time viewers is now over 65 
Only 7% of adults say nearly all their TV/video watching is through traditional TV channels 
Sales 
Linear national TV ad sales: $36.8bn in 2024 (-2% YoY)
Linear TV accounted for 13% of total U.S. ad spend in 2024 — surpassed by digital back in 2016
Linear ad sales fell -4.5% in 2025 and are projected to fall another -5.9% in 2026
In Q3 2025: Linear national TV was down -5.8%, with cable networks specifically down -5.8% and English broadcast down -10.5%
Top linear TV spending verticals (2024): Finance (12%), Pharma (12%), Personal Care (11%) — together 35% of spend
Cord-Cutting
Traditional MVPD (cable/satellite) households are declining at 5–6 million per year
Virtual MVPDs (YouTube TV, Hulu Live, etc.) are not growing fast enough to offset linear declines
Approximately 75% of national TV ad dollars now come from upfront deals (up from 60% five years ago)

Regulatory Environment (Beamz)
Consolidation/Bundling
Paramount acquisition of HBO after already acquiring Skydance
FCC friendly to acquisitions- PwC’s recently published US Deals 2026 Outlook noted a major uptick in media and telecom transaction volume and value over the past six months “characterized by headline-grabbing megadeals, increased consolidation within streaming, and a pronounced shift toward profitability and scale — underscoring a renewed confidence among both strategic and financial buyers in deploying capital.”
The FCC has shown flexibility by granting waivers to traditional ownership caps (e.g., Nexstar Media now exceeds the 39% cap, reaching 54.5% of U.S. TV households), reflecting the impact of digital competition and market consolidation.
Data Privacy Regulation (CCPA, CPRA, State Laws) leading to focus on measurement solutions that are compliant with the updated laws
Nearly 20 states now have comprehensive privacy statutes, requiring media companies to comply with a patchwork of state-specific rules.
CCPA and CPRA amendments effective January 1, 2026, require:
Formal risk assessments and cybersecurity audits.
Prescriptive measures on automated decision-making technology.
Clear consumer notifications at or before data collection, especially for sensitive or connected device data.
