UPFRONTS
ROUND-UP
Approx. 75% of national TV
ad dollars come from
upfront deals (either
broadcast year or calendar
year) these days, up from
60% five years ago.
Five owners dominate
national TV market share:
Comcast, Fox Corp,
Paramount Global, Warner
Bros Discovery and Walt
Disney. These five
companies control 78% of
the market.
The top five networks are
the big 4 broadcast
networks (NBC, FOX, CBS,
ABC) and ESPN, Disney’s
flagship sports network.
Ad spend in Auto,
Entertainment and Retail
categories are forecast to
decline in 2025. On the
other hand, Tech, Travel
and Finance are expected
to see the strongest growth.

LINEAR TV REMAINS
RELEVANT FOR REACH,
PARTICULARLY IN SPORTS
Linear tv is struggling
to keep up
The amount of households holding onto their cable cords is dwindling and
expected to continue its decline in the coming years. Cable networks’
weekly reach fell from 54% to 30% in last five years, and the median age of
broadcast prime is now over 65 years old.
Kantar’s annual global report found that only 7% of adults say that nearly
all of their TV/video watching is through TV channels.
The TV market share was surpassed by digital media in 2016 and in 2024
accounted for only 13% of total ad sales in the US.
Sports is the only category seeing growth in
terms of time spent watching.
